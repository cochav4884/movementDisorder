Education & Research

Access to reliable, research-backed information is essential for understanding movement disorders and making informed decisions about care and treatment.

Below are trusted organizations, medical institutions, and community programs that provide educational materials, research updates, and patient support.

Featured Organizations

International Parkinson and Movement Disorder Society
Offers clinical tools, research updates, and global education initiatives.

UCSF Movement Disorders Center
Provides patient education, treatment guidance, and clinical insights.

Rush University Movement Disorders Program
Offers clinical expertise, research findings, and patient-focused education.

PMD Alliance
Focuses on community-based education, support programs, and connection.

Types of Educational Resources

Clinical Research

Peer-reviewed studies and medical trials that explore causes, treatments, and progression of movement disorders.

Patient Education

Easy-to-understand materials designed to help individuals and families navigate diagnosis, treatment, and daily care.

Community Programs

Workshops, webinars, and support groups that provide both education and connection.

Why Education Matters

Understanding symptoms, treatment options, and lifestyle strategies can help individuals feel more in control of their condition and improve communication with healthcare providers.

Motus Collective encourages ongoing learning as a key part of managing neurological movement disorders.
