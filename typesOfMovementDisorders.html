Tremors

Tremors (General) ▸

Tremors are involuntary, rhythmic shaking movements that may occur at rest or during movement and can have many underlying causes. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Essential Tremor ▸

A common neurological disorder causing shaking during voluntary movement, most often affecting the hands. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Resting Tremor ▸

Occurs when muscles are relaxed and decreases with voluntary movement. Commonly associated with Parkinson’s disease. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Action Tremor ▸

Occurs during voluntary movement and includes postural and intention tremors. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Intention Tremor ▸

Becomes worse as a person approaches a target and is linked to cerebellar dysfunction. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Postural Tremor ▸

Occurs when holding a position against gravity, such as holding the arms outstretched. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Parkinsonian Disorders

Parkinson’s Disease ▸

A progressive neurological disorder affecting movement, balance, and coordination. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Progressive Supranuclear Palsy (PSP) ▸

Atypical parkinsonian disorder affecting balance, eye movement, and speech. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Multiple System Atrophy (MSA) ▸

A progressive disorder affecting movement and autonomic functions such as blood pressure. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Dystonia

Dystonia ▸

Involuntary muscle contractions that cause abnormal movements or postures. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Focal Dystonia ▸

Affects a single body part, such as the neck, hand, or face. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Generalized Dystonia ▸

Affects multiple areas of the body and often begins in childhood. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Ataxia

Ataxia ▸

A disorder affecting coordination, balance, and speech due to cerebellar dysfunction. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Spinocerebellar Ataxia ▸

A group of inherited ataxia with progressive coordination loss. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Friedreich’s Ataxia ▸

A genetic condition affecting movement, speech, and heart function. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Other Movement Disorders

Tourette Syndrome ▸

A neurological disorder involving involuntary motor and vocal tics. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Tardive Dyskinesia ▸

Involuntary repetitive movements often caused by long-term medication use. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Myoclonus ▸

Sudden, brief involuntary muscle jerks. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Restless Legs Syndrome ▸

Uncomfortable sensations in the legs with an urge to move, especially at rest. Click on circle button below to learn more.

Wilson’s Disease ▸

A genetic disorder causing copper buildup that affects movement and liver function. Click on circle button below to learn more.
