Understanding Movement Disorders

Movement disorders are neurological conditions that affect the speed, coordination, or control of voluntary and involuntary movement. This guide highlights major types of movement disorders and provides trusted medical resources for further learning.

Symptoms Quick Guide

Tremor / Shaking

Rhythmic shaking of hands, head, or voice.

Muscle Rigidity

Stiffness and resistance to movement.

Loss of Coordination

Difficulty walking or maintaining balance.

Slow Movement

Slowed voluntary movements.

Involuntary Movements

Sudden jerks or spasms.

Movement Disorder Categories

Tremor Disorders ▶

Tremors are rhythmic, involuntary shaking movements affecting various parts of the body.

Essential Tremor • Postural Tremor • Intention Tremor • Resting Tremor

Parkinsonian Disorders ▶

Parkinsonian disorders involve slowed movement, rigidity, tremor, and impaired balance.

Parkinson’s Disease • Multiple System Atrophy • Progressive Supranuclear Palsy

Dystonia ▶

Dystonia causes sustained or repetitive muscle contractions resulting in abnormal postures or twisting movements.

Focal Dystonia • Segmental Dystonia • Generalized Dystonia

Ataxia Disorders ▶

Ataxia refers to impaired coordination and balance due to cerebellar dysfunction.

Spinocerebellar Ataxia • Friedreich’s Ataxia

Other Movement Disorders ▶

Tourette Syndrome: Involuntary motor and vocal tics. Click the circle to learn more.

Tardive Dyskinesia: Involuntary repetitive movements, often medication-induced. Click the circle to learn more.

Myoclonus: Sudden, brief involuntary muscle jerks. Click the circle to learn more.

Restless Legs Syndrome: Uncomfortable leg sensations with an urge to move. Click the circle to learn more.

Wilson's Disease: Genetic copper buildup affecting movement and liver function. Click the circle to learn more.
