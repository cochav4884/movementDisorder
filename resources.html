Movement Disorder Research & References

Each card below summarizes a specific movement disorder and provides the key references for further research.

Tremor Disorders

Tremors are rhythmic, involuntary shaking movements affecting various parts of the body. Common subtypes include essential tremor, postural tremor, intention tremor, and resting tremor.

References
  • National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke. (2023). Tremor Information Page.
  • Mayo Clinic. (2024). Essential Tremor Overview.

Parkinsonian Disorders

Parkinsonian disorders involve slowed movement, rigidity, tremor, and impaired balance. Includes Parkinson’s disease, multiple system atrophy, and progressive supranuclear palsy.

References
  • Parkinson’s Foundation. (2024). Understanding Parkinson’s Disease.
  • National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke. (2023). Parkinson’s Disease Information Page.

Dystonia

Dystonia causes sustained or repetitive muscle contractions resulting in abnormal postures or twisting movements. Includes focal, segmental, and generalized dystonia.

References
  • National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke. (2023). Dystonia Overview.
  • Cleveland Clinic. (2023). Dystonia: Symptoms and Treatments.

Ataxia Disorders

Ataxia refers to impaired coordination and balance due to cerebellar dysfunction. Includes spinocerebellar ataxia and Friedreich’s ataxia.

References
  • National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke. (2023). Ataxia Information Page.
  • Mayo Clinic. (2024). Ataxia Overview.

Other Movement Disorders

This category includes Tourette Syndrome, Tardive Dyskinesia, Myoclonus, Restless Legs Syndrome, and Wilson's Disease.

References
  • Tourette Association of America. (2023). Tourette Syndrome Information.
  • Cleveland Clinic. (2023). Tardive Dyskinesia Overview.
  • National Institute of Neurological Disorders and Stroke. (2023). Myoclonus Information Page.
  • Restless Legs Syndrome Foundation. (2022). RLS Overview.
  • Wilson Disease Association. (2023). Wilson's Disease Overview.
